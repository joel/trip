Sunrise at Seceda

October 14, 2023 • Dolomites, Italy

+12

The air at 2,500 meters tastes different.

We started the ascent at 4:30 AM. The world was still a silent, monochromatic shadow as our headlamps cut through the crisp mountain air. Every breath felt like a renewal, a sharp reminder of the altitude and the raw power of the Dolomites.

As we reached the ridgeline of Seceda, the first hints of amber began to bleed through the deep indigo of the horizon. It wasn't a sudden burst, but a slow, intentional unveiling. The jagged peaks, famous for their razor-sharp silhouettes, began to catch the light—turning from grey to purple, then finally to a burning, brilliant orange.

"Nature is not a place to visit. It is home." — The realization hit differently as the sun finally cleared the peaks.

I spent hours just sitting there, watching the shadows retract across the valley floor. There's a profound stillness that comes with being above the clouds. You realize that while the world below is waking up to noise and schedules, up here, the only clock is the movement of light.
